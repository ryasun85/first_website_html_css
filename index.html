1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

GDPR is a European Union set of rules (agreement) for storing and manipulating personal data. The main reason this was put in place is to give individuals more control over their data and being able to assess how it is being processed. Businesses from outside of EU are also obligated to follow the rules if they operate within the EU.
The data protection is very beneficial to individuals who can easily request to be removed from databases of businesses. It solves a lot of issues with for example unwanted email communication. It has a huge impact on delivering digital project very often causing delays and also increase in price for the product for an end user.
Having to follow new GDPR restriction businesses often have to have someone overlooking this which is an additional cost of employing another person. For businesses doing SaaS this can cause delays at testing stage. Testing often has to be done with data which now has to be anonymised/encrypted before being used in the testing.

1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Copyright is put in place to make sure original work is protected. As images, text, videos are often someone's intellectual property this helps to make sure people get credit for their work and its not used without their permission. If we are looking to use someone else’s work - for example graphic - we need to ask for permission, credit the person or pay them for using their work.
Very important rule for creating website these days is also their accessibility. We should make sure that websites are easily accessible for people with disabilities for example people with poor vision. (Looking at color contrast could be beneficial)

1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using web pages and how we can overcome Them

When we think about accessibility is not just for people with disabilities but we also have to make sure our website can be easily used on different devices like for example mobile phones or TVs. Is worth having a think about who will be our audience first to be able to plan ahead.
